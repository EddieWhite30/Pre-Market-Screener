Reference
IV rank — implied volatility
IV rank measures where current implied volatility sits relative to its 52-week range. A rank of 0 means IV is at its lowest point of the year — contracts are cheapest. A rank of 100 means IV is at its highest — contracts are most expensive.
The market's forecast of how much a stock will move — priced into every options contract.
0 – 30
Cheap premium
Favorable for buying options — best risk/reward on entry
30 – 60
Moderate premium
Tradeable — setup quality must be higher to justify cost
60 – 100
Expensive premium
Be cautious — stock must move significantly more to profit
Where to find it  ·  marketchameleon.com → search ticker → look for IV30 % Rank in the top data block
ADR % — average daily range
ADR % measures how much a stock typically moves from its daily low to daily high, expressed as a percentage of price. It tells you whether a stock moves enough to make options profitable — even cheap premium loses if the stock barely moves.
For Mag 7 names, ADR is a secondary filter — use it as a tiebreaker when two tickers have similar IV rank.
below 1.5%
Slow mover
May not move enough to overcome premium cost
1.5% – 3%
Decent mover
Workable for ORB — clean setup required
above 3%
Strong mover
Ideal range — enough movement to generate premium expansion
Where to find it  ·  Search "Average Daily Range" in TradingView indicators — use a script that locks to daily timeframe data regardless of chart timeframe
